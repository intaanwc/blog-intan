## Overview

The USAID Momentum Private Healthcare Delivery project, implemented by Jhpiego, aimed to strengthen private sector engagement in maternal and newborn health services in Indonesia. The programme focused on improving the quality and reach of private healthcare providers.

## Role and Responsibilities

As a Project Assistant, I supported programme implementation and monitoring activities:

- Assisted in documenting field activities and compiling monitoring data for reporting
- Supported coordination with private healthcare providers and local stakeholders
- Contributed to the preparation of progress reports aligned with USAID reporting requirements
- Participated in programme review meetings and supported follow-up actions

## Context

Working within a USAID-funded programme gave me direct exposure to the structures and reporting expectations of large bilateral donors — a valuable foundation for understanding how global health funding flows and what accountability looks like in practice.

## Key Takeaway

Even in a support role, the exposure to programme design, USAID compliance frameworks, and the operational realities of private sector health delivery was formative. It reinforced my interest in the intersection of health policy and implementation — where plans meet practice.
