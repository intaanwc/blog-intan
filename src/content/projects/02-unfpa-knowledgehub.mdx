## Overview

The KnowledgeHub for Reproductive Health Indonesia was a UNFPA-led initiative designed to centralise, curate, and disseminate evidence on reproductive health for practitioners, policymakers, and communities across Indonesia. As Project Management Officer, I was responsible for day-to-day project operations across a multi-donor portfolio.

## Scope of Work

This was a cross-functional role requiring simultaneous management of:

- **Project coordination** across UNFPA country office teams and implementing partners
- **Data analysis** on reproductive health indicators and programme reach
- **Survey design and field implementation** to capture community-level insights
- **Donor reporting** for DFAT Australia, Government of Japan, and the Government of Canada
- **Budgeting and financial monitoring** to ensure expenditure aligned with work plans
- **Knowledge management** — curating resources and maintaining the platform's content quality
- **Hiring and HR** — supporting recruitment and onboarding of project staff

## Community Engagement

A significant part of this work involved direct engagement with communities and local health cadres, ensuring the KnowledgeHub reflected ground-level realities rather than just institutional perspectives. Community feedback loops shaped content curation priorities.

## Reflections

Managing a knowledge platform across a country as diverse as Indonesia underscored how important localisation is — a resource that works in urban Java may need significant adaptation for rural Papua. This role shaped my thinking about evidence translation and the gap between data production and practical use.
