## Overview

As part of the Clinton Health Access Initiative's (CHAI) Indonesia country programme, I supported and led efforts to improve vaccine access and immunisation coverage at the national and sub-national level. My work spanned programme management, stakeholder coordination, and data-driven decision making.

## Key Contributions

- Coordinated with the Ministry of Health and provincial governments to align immunisation strategies with national targets
- Managed programme budgets and donor reporting cycles, ensuring accountability across funding streams
- Conducted field visits and needs assessments across provinces to identify barriers to vaccine access
- Produced evidence-based programme updates and policy briefs shared with government and development partners
- Contributed to Cold Chain Equipment Optimisation (CCEOP) and supply chain strengthening efforts

## Impact

The programme supported Indonesia in maintaining and expanding routine immunisation coverage, with targeted interventions in underserved areas. Work under this initiative contributed to measurable improvements in cold-chain functionality and last-mile delivery in pilot districts.

## Reflections

Working at the intersection of public health systems and operations taught me how critical the interplay between data, relationships, and execution is in global health programmes. No two districts present the same challenge — and that variation is what makes this work endlessly interesting.
