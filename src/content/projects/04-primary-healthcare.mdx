## Overview

Before transitioning into programme management, I worked as a registered midwife across multiple healthcare settings in Malang, East Java — including a government hospital, a public health centre (Puskesmas), and a private practice. This was where my clinical foundation was built, and where I first encountered the operational realities of frontline health service delivery.

## Clinical Scope

Day-to-day responsibilities spanned a wide range of maternal and child health services:

- Antenatal and postnatal care
- Normal and assisted deliveries
- Family planning counselling and service provision
- Neonatal care and early childhood health monitoring
- Reproductive health education and community outreach

## Cold-Chain Vaccine Management

Beyond clinical duties, I took on responsibility for cold-chain vaccine stock management — ensuring temperature-controlled storage, monitoring stock levels, and coordinating replenishment with district health authorities. This experience directly informed my later work on vaccine programme management at CHAI.

## Revitalising the Posyandu

One initiative I'm particularly proud of is re-energising the local Integrated Service Post (Posyandu) — a community-based health post that had fallen into low utilisation. Through community outreach, coordination with local cadres, and improved scheduling, we saw renewed engagement from mothers and families in the area.

## Reflections

Clinical work as a midwife shaped everything that came after. Understanding how care is actually experienced — by a mother in a rural health centre, by a community health worker with limited supplies — gives a grounding that no policy document can fully replicate. I carry that perspective into every programme I work on.
